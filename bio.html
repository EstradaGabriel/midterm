Childhood Years

1I was born in Quezon City, Novaliches, but spent my childhood in Manaoag, Pangasinan.2 My parents were hardworking entrepreneurs who managed multiple businesses.3 As the eldest of four siblings, my childhood was full of responsibilities and fun.4 I loved playing with my neighborhood friends until late at night.5 Our family’s computer shop became my playground, where I developed an early interest in computers.6 I also enjoyed building Lego sets and collecting toy cars.7 Watching Tagalog-dubbed anime with my father became one of my favorite bonding moments.8 He used to watch anime when he was younger, which influenced my love for it.9 Family trips to San Fabian Beach were always a highlight of my childhood.10 One of my fondest memories was swimming with my cousins when Mama Alice visited.11 My lolo and lola also made those vacations special with their stories.12 In school, I was fascinated by science, especially space exploration.13 I even joined a chess tournament in grade 5 and won a bronze medal.14 My mother always called me "malikot" because I was full of energy.15 I struggled with speaking up, but I slowly overcame it.16 One of the best childhood moments was watching the movie *Cars*, which sparked my love for automobiles.17 I dreamed of becoming a pilot back then, inspired by the idea of adventure.18 Looking back, my childhood was simple yet meaningful.19 It shaped many of the interests I still hold today.20 My parents’ hard work also inspired me to be ambitious and independent.

Teenage Years

1 My teenage years were filled with self-discovery and challenges.2 I attended Our Lady of Manaoag Innovative School for junior high and ASAP for senior high.3 I was lucky to have a close group of friends from childhood. 4 My high school principal was an extraordinary teacher who inspired me.5 I always looked forward to foundation day because it was a break from academics.6 I joined the school COMELEC as vice governor and helped organize elections.7 I also joined the arts and photography club to explore my creativity.8 When the pandemic hit, I managed our family’s computer shop.9 Handling printing, photocopying, and tarpaulin layouts taught me responsibility.10 It was my first real experience in the working world.11 However, I also faced personal struggles like self-doubt and depression.12 There were times I questioned whether I could achieve my goals.13 My first heartbreak also happened during this time after pursuing someone for three years.14 It was painful, but it taught me important lessons about love and respect.15 Cycling became my escape, helping me clear my mind.16 Photography also became a passion, especially capturing scenic places.17 I enjoyed uploading my photos to Google Maps to help others navigate.18 Cooking, especially making egg fried rice at midnight, became another hobby.19 I started believing in life lessons like “it is what it is.”20 Through all the ups and downs, I grew stronger and more independent.

College Years

1 I am currently a BSIT student at the University of the East.2 My mother also studied at UE, making it special for me.3 Growing up around computers made IT a natural choice.4 Adjusting to college life was smooth, though making friends was a bit challenging.5 Over time, I built meaningful connections with classmates.6 My college days feel repetitive but rewarding.7 I enjoy hanging out with my classmates after each semester ends.8 One of the most interesting things I learned was soldering PCB boards.9 Fixing and modifying mice switches became a small side hobby.10 My biggest challenge in college is overcoming procrastination. 11 I’ve learned to manage my time better with a weekly schedule.12 I am not part of any school organizations, preferring to focus on academics.13 However, I try to balance studies with social life and hobbies.14 My main goal after graduation is to find a stable job.15 I want to support my family so my parents can finally rest.16 Beyond that, my long-term dream is to build a man cave underground in my own house.17 I also want to own my dream cars someday.18 These dreams keep me motivated to work hard.19 College is teaching me the importance of discipline and perseverance.20 It’s not just about getting a degree but building a future for myself and my family.
